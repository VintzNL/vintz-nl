This mid-century aluminium pedal bin is proof that even the most humble household object can be quietly beautiful. A tapering cylinder of polished aluminium sits on three splayed tubular legs, lifting it off the floor like a little piece of space-age furniture. A slim lever arches across the domed lid to a foot pedal below — press it, and the lid rises with that satisfying mechanical certainty they simply do not build into bins anymore.

It is the engineering that charms. Everything is honest and exposed: the riveted lever, the angled legs capped with their original feet, the gently softened seams of hand-finished metal. There is no plastic, no branding clutter — just a designer's confident answer to the question of how a waste bin should stand in a room.

The aluminium has aged into a soft, brushed glow, scattered with the faint scratches and cloudy patches that come from decades of real use. We describe it candidly as in good condition: structurally sound and fully working, with the honest surface character of a genuine vintage piece rather than a polished reproduction.

Objects like this have a second life today as sculptural storage — a bathroom or kitchen bin with real presence, a magazine holder, or simply a conversation piece on its tripod stance. It sits happily among our more characterful industrial finds, like our Faema E61 espresso machine, and the rest of our curated vintage objects.

Like every piece at Vintz, it was hand-picked for character and condition rather than restored, then shipped worldwide in a custom wooden crate, fully insured. Keep it doing its honest job, or let it stand as the small sculptural surprise it really is — either way, good design earns its place.

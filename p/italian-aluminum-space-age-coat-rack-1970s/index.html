This is what happens when Italian industrial design meets the optimism of the Space Age. Standing at an imposing 183 centimetres, this aluminium coat rack rises from its slender tripod base like a modernist tree — a constellation of sleek, disc-topped arms branching outward, each one ready to receive a coat, a hat, or a scarf with equal grace.

The combination of steel and aluminium gives the piece both structural confidence and visual lightness. The cool, reflective surface of the aluminium catches ambient light, creating a presence that shifts throughout the day. The proportions are deliberately dramatic — tall and narrow, it occupies minimal floor space while commanding maximum visual attention.

This example is in good condition with only minor signs of use. The aluminium retains its characteristic lustre, and the tripod base remains stable and true. For a piece that has stood sentinel in an Italian hallway for over fifty years, it wears its age remarkably well.

The late 1960s and 1970s were a golden period for Italian object design. Designers were experimenting with industrial materials — aluminium, plastics, chromed steel — to create domestic objects that felt futuristic without being cold. This coat rack belongs firmly in that tradition, alongside pieces by Kartell, Artemide, and the Memphis precursors.

In a contemporary entryway, this piece does double duty: it holds your outerwear and it signals your design sensibility before guests have taken a single step into the rest of the house.

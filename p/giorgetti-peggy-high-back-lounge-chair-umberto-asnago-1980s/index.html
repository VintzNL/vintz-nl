The Peggy chair is one of those designs that announces its Italian pedigree the moment you enter the room. Created by Umberto Asnago for Giorgetti — one of Italy's most distinguished furniture houses — it stands tall and self-assured, with the kind of high-backed silhouette that turns a corner of any room into a private retreat.

The lacquered wooden frame is the star here. Giorgetti's legendary woodworking tradition is on full display: the grain is subtly visible beneath the lacquer, adding warmth and depth to the sleek form. The leather upholstery invites you to sit, while the frame's proportions keep you sitting upright with an almost regal posture. This is comfort with composure.

This example is in good vintage condition. The lacquer on the back shows some discolouration — a patina that speaks to decades of life in a real home. The leather has softened naturally over time. These are not flaws; they are evidence of a chair that has been lived with and appreciated.

Umberto Asnago's collaboration with Giorgetti, which began in the 1980s, produced some of the most refined Italian furniture of the late twentieth century. The Peggy sits within a tradition that connects Gio Ponti's post-war elegance to the more expressive Italian design of the 1980s and 1990s.

Whether placed in a living room, a library, or a well-appointed office, the Peggy commands its space without overwhelming it. It is the kind of chair that makes people pause, look, and then ask where you found it.

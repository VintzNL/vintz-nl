While his Maralunga sofa claimed the spotlight and the Compasso d'Oro, Vico Magistretti was quietly working on something more intimate. The Portovenere, designed in 1989 and manufactured by Cassina, is a two-seater that trades the Maralunga's architectural drama for a subtler kind of sophistication — named, fittingly, after the picturesque Ligurian coastal town whose curved cliffs may well have inspired the sofa's distinctive winged backrest.

The leather is supple and warm, with the kind of natural grain that only Italian hide processed by Cassina's exacting standards can achieve. The seat cushions are generous without being overstuffed, and the backrest curves forward at each end to create adjustable headrests — wings that can be positioned upright for reading or conversation, or folded down for a more relaxed silhouette. At one hundred and seventy-five centimeters wide, it is perfectly proportioned for two people.

This example shows honest wear consistent with decades of use. The leather has developed a patina that deepens its character, with the kind of surface variation that tells you this sofa has been lived with, not merely displayed. The structure remains solid, the adjustment mechanisms work smoothly, and the overall form retains the crispness of Magistretti's original intent.

Magistretti's relationship with Cassina began in the 1960s and produced some of the most important furniture of the late twentieth century. He was famous for arriving at their workshops with sketches drawn on the backs of bus tickets — ideas so clear in his mind that the most minimal notation was sufficient. Cassina, for their part, had been revolutionizing Italian furniture since 1927 and possessed the manufacturing sophistication to realize even Magistretti's most deceptively simple concepts.

The Portovenere is exactly the kind of sofa that improves a room without announcing itself. Its lines are clean enough for a minimalist apartment, its leather warm enough for a book-lined study. It is a designer sofa for people who care about design but would rather sit in it than talk about it.

The DS-11 is De Sede at its most confident. Six modular sections in warm cognac patchwork leather, each one a self-contained statement that becomes something greater when combined. This is the sofa that defined an era of Swiss luxury — bold, flexible, and built with the kind of material honesty that makes lesser furniture feel apologetic.

The patchwork leather upholstery is a De Sede signature — panels of carefully matched hide stitched together in a geometric pattern that celebrates the craft of working with natural material. The cognac tone has a depth and warmth that comes from genuine leather properly treated, with subtle variations between panels that give the surface visual texture and life. This particular set has been professionally restored to bring back its original beauty while preserving the character of four decades of life.

The restoration work is excellent. The leather is supple and clean, the stitching secure throughout, and the overall condition is very good. The six modular sections sit on solid wooden frames and can be configured in multiple arrangements — a long sofa, an L-shape, separate seating groups, or whatever your space demands. Each section measures approximately 65 by 65 centimetres with a seat height of 38 centimetres.

De Sede launched the DS-11 in the early 1970s as part of their expanding modular range. Where the DS-46 and DS-47 showcased thick neck leather, the DS-11 demonstrated that patchwork construction could be elevated from economy to luxury. The meticulous matching of leather panels and precise hand-stitching transformed a practical approach into an aesthetic statement.

A six-piece DS-11 in this condition is increasingly difficult to find. It transforms a living room, a loft space, or a private office into somewhere you genuinely want to spend time — surrounded by material quality that you can feel every time you sit down.

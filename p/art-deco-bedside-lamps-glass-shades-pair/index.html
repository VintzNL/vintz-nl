Some lamps light a room; these two give it a wink. This pair of **Art Deco bedside lamps** from the 1930s carries everything the era loved in miniature: a chromed swan-neck arm, a weighted oval base, and a glass shade decorated with playful abstract motifs in red, blue and ochre.

The shades are the showpiece. Hand-finished in a soft cream tone, each one catches the light differently — by day they sit like small sculptures on a nightstand, by night they cast a warm, intimate glow that suits a bedroom or reading corner perfectly.

The chrome arms curve in the optimistic, streamlined spirit of the 1930s, and the enamelled bases show exactly the kind of patina you want from a piece this age: honest wear that tells you these lamps have lived, without taking anything away from their charm.

True pairs of Art Deco bedside lamps with their original decorated shades intact are increasingly hard to find — glass this thin rarely survives ninety years. These two did, together.

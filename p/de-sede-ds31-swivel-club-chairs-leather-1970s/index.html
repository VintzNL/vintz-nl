A matched pair of De Sede DS-31 club chairs from 1975 — the kind of furniture that turns a living room into somewhere you actually want to be. Low-slung, leather-wrapped, and mounted on swivel bases that rotate with the smoothness you only get from Swiss engineering done right.

The leather is finished with De Sede's signature hand-stitched whipseams — those distinctive edge details that immediately identify serious Swiss craftsmanship. The hide has aged beautifully over five decades, developing a warm, lived-in character that new leather simply cannot match. Each chair sits low and wide, inviting you to settle in rather than perch. The swivel bases add a practical playfulness — pivot toward the fireplace, turn to face a guest, or simply rotate for the pleasure of sitting in a chair that moves with you.

Both chairs are in very good condition with only minor signs of use. The leather is supple throughout, the stitching is intact, the swivel mechanisms operate smoothly, and the overall structures are solid and stable. They match each other beautifully in tone and patina, clearly having lived together in the same space throughout their history.

De Sede produced the DS-31 at their Klingnau workshop during the mid-1970s, a period when the company was defining what luxury seating could mean. The club chair version of the DS-31 took the series' clean lines and added a more intimate, enveloping silhouette — lower to the ground, wider in the seat, designed for relaxation rather than conversation. The addition of a swivel base was a distinctly 1970s move, reflecting the era's fascination with flexible, informal seating.

Finding a matched pair in this condition is uncommon. Together, they anchor a room with the confidence of pieces that were built to last and have proven it — fifty years of quiet luxury, ready for fifty more.

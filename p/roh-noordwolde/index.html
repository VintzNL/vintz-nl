Rohe Noordwolde is one of those names that immediately signals a particular kind of Dutch craftsmanship. Based in the Frisian village of Noordwolde — historically the centre of Dutch basket and rattan weaving — the company produced furniture that elevated a humble material into something genuinely elegant.

This coffee table exemplifies that transformation. The rattan frame is handwoven with the precision and consistency that comes from generations of accumulated skill. The natural material creates an airy, open structure that feels light and inviting, while the glass top provides a practical, smooth surface. The combination is classic mid-century Dutch design: warm, functional, and effortlessly stylish.

The table is in good condition with its original rattan weave intact. The natural fibres have developed a warm, golden patina that only improves with age. The glass top is clear and unblemished. At 42 centimetres tall and 74 by 45 centimetres across, it offers a practical surface area while maintaining the light, unobtrusive presence that makes rattan furniture so versatile.

Noordwolde's rattan weaving tradition dates back to the nineteenth century, when the village became a hub for this specialised craft. Rohe was among the most prominent manufacturers, producing designs that found their way into homes across the Netherlands and beyond. Their work represents a uniquely Dutch contribution to mid-century design — one that prioritises natural materials, honest construction, and quiet beauty over flashier gestures.

In a living room, a sunroom, or a covered terrace, this Rohe coffee table brings warmth, texture, and a piece of Dutch design heritage. It is light in weight but substantial in character — the kind of piece that instantly makes a space feel like home.

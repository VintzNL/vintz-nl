Mohair changes everything. On the F588, Pierre Frey's mohair plush transforms Geoffrey Harcourt's already exceptional lounge chair into a sensory experience that operates on a level most furniture never reaches.

The warm yellowish velvet does not simply cover the chair; it elevates it. Mohair has a natural lustre that shifts with the light, creating an interplay of shadow and sheen across the F588's generous curves. The classic button detailing, a signature of this model, creates gentle valleys in the fabric that deepen the three-dimensional quality of the upholstery.

The F588 shares DNA with Harcourt's celebrated F510 but distinguishes itself through a more generous seat and a slightly more reclined posture. The swivel base rotates smoothly, allowing the sitter to follow conversation, catch a view, or simply let their gaze wander. The high back provides the sheltering effect that Harcourt's designs are known for.

Harcourt designed the F588 during his most productive period at Artifort in the 1960s, when the Maastricht-based company was at the forefront of European furniture innovation. Working alongside design director Kho Liang Ie, Harcourt developed a design vocabulary that balanced sculptural ambition with genuine ergonomic care.

This chair promises something that few objects in a modern home can deliver: pure aesthetic pleasure combined with deep physical comfort. It is the kind of piece that becomes the favourite seat in the house, the one everyone gravitates toward.

A cushion frozen in fiberglass. That is the paradox at the heart of this Roche Bobois cocktail table — a sculptural tribute to the legendary Mah Jong sofa, rendered in a material that captures the soft, billowing form while making it permanent and structural.

The table's surface suggests the gentle undulation of a plush cushion, but the fiberglass body is rigid and surprisingly lightweight. A glass top sits atop the sculpted base, creating a usable surface that hovers above the organic form beneath. It is a playful, postmodern gesture — the kind of design that invites a double take and rewards it with a smile.

This example is in good original condition, with light wear consistent with regular use over two decades. The fiberglass structure is solid and free from cracks, and the glass top is intact and clear. At 22 centimetres tall and 94 centimetres square, it provides generous surface area at a comfortable coffee table height.

Roche Bobois has been commissioning reinterpretations of its classic designs for decades, and this table draws directly from the Mah Jong sofa — designed by Hans Hopfer in 1971 and still in production today. Where the original Mah Jong celebrates flexibility and softness, this table captures that spirit in a frozen moment. It is a piece that understands its source material deeply and plays with it intelligently.

In a living room, this table becomes both functional surface and sculptural object. It pairs naturally with a Mah Jong sofa but works equally well alongside more conventional seating — bringing a sense of wit, luxury, and French design confidence to any arrangement.

A frozen wingbeat captured in crystal. This Arte Vannes vase from the 1970s transforms French glassmaking tradition into something approaching sculpture, a centrepiece that commands attention from every angle.

The winged form is both delicate and powerful, rising from its pedestal with the kind of confidence that only hand-blown crystal can achieve. Each facet, each curve has been precisely shaped and polished by hand, honouring the centuries-old tradition of Vannes-le-Chatel. The organic lines flow into a harmonious whole, light playing through the crystal in ways that shift throughout the day.

This example is in excellent condition, with no chips, no cracks, and the full clarity of the original crystal preserved. The proportions are generous at 62 centimetres deep, making it a true centrepiece rather than a shelf ornament.

Arte Vannes traces its roots to the eastern Lorraine region of France, an area that has been synonymous with master glassmaking since the eighteenth century. Vannes-le-Chatel remains home to Europe's largest glassmaking school, and the craftsmanship of its artisans is evident in every surface of this piece. The 1970s saw these ateliers push beyond traditional forms into more sculptural territory, and this winged vase represents that creative ambition at its most successful.

On a console, a dining table, or a mantelpiece, this vase does not merely decorate a space. It transforms it, catching and refracting light like a prism of French artisanal heritage.

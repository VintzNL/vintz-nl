There is a lineage of Italian chairmaking that runs through the coastal town of Chiavari, and these ladder-back chairs sit squarely within it. Attributed to Paolo Buffa and dating to the 1950s, they embody a particular kind of Italian elegance — restrained, refined, and utterly confident in its simplicity.

The classic ladder-back design is executed in high-quality wood that has developed a warm, characterful patina over seven decades. The seats feature a hand-applied rose motif, adding an artistic, almost romantic quality that softens the chair's architectural lines. The proportions are impeccable: tall, slender backs that lend vertical grace to any dining table or hallway.

Both chairs are in good condition, with light signs of use that only enhance their authenticity. The wood is structurally sound, the joints remain tight, and the hand-painted motifs are well preserved. These are chairs that have lived, and they are better for it.

Paolo Buffa (1903–1970) was one of the most important figures in mid-century Italian furniture design. Working primarily in Milan, he developed a distinctive style that bridged the gap between neoclassical elegance and modernist clarity. His association with the Chiavari tradition — the lightweight, beautifully proportioned chairs that originated in Liguria — produced some of the most sought-after seating of the postwar period.

Whether flanking a console table, pulled up to a writing desk, or placed at a dining table, this pair brings a quiet, cultivated beauty to any interior. They are the kind of chairs that make a room feel considered.

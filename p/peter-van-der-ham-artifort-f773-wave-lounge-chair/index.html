Some pieces of furniture invite you to sit down. This Leolux loveseat practically insists. With its oversized, petal-shaped cushions cradled in a wooden frame, it combines the playful spirit of 1980s Dutch design with a level of comfort that borders on indulgent.

Leolux, founded in 1934 in Venlo, the Netherlands, has spent nine decades blending innovation with traditional craftsmanship. The Model 785 exemplifies that ethos perfectly. The wooden frame provides architectural structure, while two generously proportioned cushions create a seating experience that is both enveloping and supportive. The silhouette is bold — almost sculptural — yet the construction is robust enough for daily use.

This example has been recently reupholstered in semi-aniline leather of exceptional quality — 2.8 millimetres thick, with the supple, buttery hand feel characteristic of premium neck leather. The rich dark brown hue deepens with time and use, developing a patina that only genuine leather can achieve. The reupholstery has given this vintage piece a second life without erasing its original character.

Leolux has remained a family-owned company throughout its history, a rarity in the furniture industry. Their designs have consistently challenged the boundary between art and function, producing pieces that are recognisable, comfortable, and built to last. The Model 785 sits firmly within that tradition — a design that could only have come from a house with deep roots in both craft and experimentation.

In a living room, a reading corner, or a bedroom, this loveseat becomes the piece everyone gravitates toward. It is design you can feel — literally and figuratively.

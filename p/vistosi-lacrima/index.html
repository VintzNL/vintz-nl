The Lacrima — Italian for teardrop — is one of those designs where the name tells you everything you need to know about the form. Produced by Vistosi, one of the most respected glassworks on the island of Murano, this pendant lamp distills centuries of Venetian glass-blowing expertise into a single, flowing silhouette.

The lamp is hand-blown Murano glass, shaped into the graceful teardrop form that gives the Lacrima its name. The glass has a luminous quality that shifts with the light source inside it — warm and enveloping when lit, cool and sculptural when off. Each piece is unique in the subtle way that only handmade objects can be: slight variations in thickness, tiny undulations in the surface, the kind of imperfections that confirm human hands were involved.

This example is in original condition, with the glass intact and the hardware complete. Vistosi pieces from this era are increasingly sought after by collectors and interior designers who appreciate the combination of artisanal craft and modernist sensibility that defines the best of mid-century Italian glass production.

Vistosi was founded in 1945 by Guglielmo Vistosi and quickly established itself as one of Murano's most innovative workshops. Where older firms like Venini leaned toward classical elegance, Vistosi embraced modernism — collaborating with designers like Gino Vistosi and Angelo Mangiarotti to create lighting that felt both timeless and distinctly contemporary. The Lacrima embodies that balance: a form so ancient it predates design itself, realized in a material so refined it could only come from Murano.

Hung alone above a dining table or in a cluster of varying heights, the Lacrima transforms a ceiling into a gallery. Its soft, diffused light creates atmosphere without drama, making it equally suited to intimate residential spaces and larger architectural settings. Pure Italian elegance in glass.

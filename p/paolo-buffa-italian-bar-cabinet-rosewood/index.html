This cabinet occupies the beautiful territory where Art Deco confidence meets mid-century modern restraint. Attributed to Paolo Buffa and dating to the 1950s, it is the kind of piece that anchors a room — not through volume, but through sheer quality of design and material.

The rosewood veneer radiates warm, reddish-brown tones that shift subtly depending on the light. The proportions are elegant: rounded edges and flowing contours replace the hard geometry typical of lesser cabinets from this period. But the true stars are the handles — slender, refined, almost sculptural objects in their own right. They transform the simple act of opening a door into something tactile and considered.

This example is in good condition with only minor signs of use. The rosewood veneer is well preserved, the interior is clean and functional, and the cabinet stands solidly on its original legs. At 137 centimetres tall, 91 centimetres wide, and 49 centimetres deep, it offers generous storage without overwhelming a space.

Paolo Buffa (1903–1970) was among the most refined furniture designers of postwar Italy. His work drew from neoclassical and Art Deco traditions while anticipating the cleaner lines of modernism. Pieces like this bar cabinet demonstrate his extraordinary ability to balance decoration and restraint — every curve, every detail serves both an aesthetic and a functional purpose.

As a bar cabinet, a display piece, or simply a beautiful storage solution, this Buffa cabinet brings history, craftsmanship, and quiet sophistication to any interior. Pieces of this quality and provenance surface rarely on the open market.

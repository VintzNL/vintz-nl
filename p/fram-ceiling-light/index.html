These two identical ceiling fixtures from the Swiss manufacturer Fram demonstrate what happens when serious glassmaking craft meets 1970s lighting design. Each lamp features a square crystal shade with a complex, flower-like texture that transforms ordinary light into something atmospheric and alive.

The crystal shades are heavy and substantial — the kind of quality that immediately registers when you hold one. The textured surface disperses light in fascinating patterns, creating refraction and reflection that shift subtly depending on the angle and intensity of the bulbs inside. Each shade sits flush against the ceiling on a round white mounting plate fitted with twin sockets, providing effective room illumination while doubling as a decorative element.

Both fixtures are in good condition and fully functional. The crystal shades are intact without chips or cracks, the mounting hardware is complete, and the electrical components accommodate standard bulbs. The glass has been cleaned to reveal its full clarity and light-dispersing character.

Fram produced these ceiling fixtures in Switzerland during the 1970s, a decade when European lighting design experienced a creative peak. The combination of geometric form — the square shade — with organic texture — the floral crystal pattern — reflects the era's fascination with natural motifs translated through modern materials and manufacturing techniques. Few of these fixtures have survived intact as a matched pair, making this set particularly appealing to collectors of vintage lighting.

Mounted in an entrance hall, a dining room, or a bedroom, these Fram ceiling lights provide warm, diffused illumination with genuine visual interest. They bring a touch of 1970s Swiss quality into a space — the kind of lighting that guests notice and remember.

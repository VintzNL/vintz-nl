Space Age design at its most refined. Gaetano Sciolari's DN/1 floor lamp distills the 1960s fascination with space exploration and futuristic living into a form that is as striking today as it was when it left the workshop more than half a century ago.

The design is characteristically Sciolari: geometric, precise, and unapologetically chrome. The polished metal surfaces reflect and multiply ambient light even before the lamp is switched on, creating a dynamic visual presence. Three E14 lamp holders provide balanced illumination while maintaining the elegant, elongated silhouette that makes this floor lamp so architecturally effective. At 163 centimetres tall, it has the stature to command a corner or anchor a seating arrangement.

This is an original vintage piece, with minor marks on the lamp base that are consistent with age and use. These subtle signs of wear confirm its authenticity and add to its character as a genuine mid-century object. The chrome plating remains bright and reflective, and the lamp is in full working order.

Gaetano Sciolari was one of Italy's most important lighting designers of the twentieth century. His work for the family company defined an era of Italian lighting design that prized high-quality materials, geometric forms, and the interplay of light and reflective surface. The DN/1 is among his most sought-after floor lamp designs, combining Space Age exuberance with the kind of restrained elegance that keeps it relevant across changing interior styles.

In a living room, a reading corner, or beside a mid-century lounge chair, this Sciolari floor lamp provides both functional illumination and sculptural beauty. It is one of those rare objects that looks as good switched off as it does when casting light across a room.

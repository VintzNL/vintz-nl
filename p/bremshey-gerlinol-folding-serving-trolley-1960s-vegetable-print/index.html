The 1960s had a way of turning everyday objects into celebrations of colour and optimism. This Bremshey Gerlinol serving trolley is a perfect example: functional, cheerful, and so vividly of its era that it practically radiates mid-century sunshine.

The two Formica trays carry a vegetable print so vivid it borders on the exuberant. Bright yellow corn, juicy oranges, and crisp green cucumbers pop against an earthy background, a colour palette that captures the period's fascination with natural abundance rendered through bold, graphic design. The brass and black metal frame provides elegant structure, its folding mechanism operating as smoothly today as it did six decades ago.

The construction is typical Bremshey quality. German engineering applied to domestic objects, resulting in something that is simultaneously practical and delightful. The folding mechanism allows the trolley to collapse flat for storage, while the two-tier tray arrangement provides ample serving surface when deployed. Every hinge, every joint reflects the manufacturing precision that defined German industrial design in the 1960s.

Bremshey, based in Solingen, Germany, produced a range of household objects that have become increasingly collectible. Their serving trolleys, particularly those with Gerlinol printed trays, represent a design sensibility that valued colour, pattern, and joy in everyday domestic life.

Whether used as intended, wheeling drinks from kitchen to garden, or repurposed as a bathroom shelf or plant stand, this trolley brings warmth, colour, and a tangible connection to one of design's most optimistic decades.

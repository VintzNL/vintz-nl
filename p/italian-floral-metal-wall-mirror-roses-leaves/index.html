This Italian floral wall mirror is unapologetically romantic. Its frame is shaped from wrought metal into a generous, undulating outline — all soft curves and scalloped edges — and then crowned with hand-formed roses and trailing leaves at the top, sides and base. It is the kind of mirror that does not just reflect a room, but decorates it, the way a sprig of real flowers would.

The metalwork is where the charm lives. Each leaf is cut and veined by hand, each little rose coiled and set so that the foliage seems to grow naturally around the glass. The finish has aged to a warm, gently darkened patina — part gilt, part shadow — that gives the whole piece an antique softness new reproductions never quite manage. A twisted cord hanger completes the period look.

The mirror plate itself carries the faint, silvery cloudiness of age in places, the honest mottling that collectors of vintage glass actively prize. It is in good condition, with only minor signs of use consistent with its years — nothing that distracts from its presence on a wall.

Florentine and Hollywood-Regency makers turned this kind of floral metalwork into a whole decorative language through the mid-twentieth century, marrying a little baroque exuberance to genuine handcraft. Hung above a console, a dressing table or a fireplace, it brings instant softness and light. It sits beautifully among the rest of our curated vintage objects, or alongside Italian decorative pieces like our Carlo Moretti Murano vases.

Like every piece at Vintz, it was hand-picked for character and condition rather than restored, then shipped worldwide in a custom wooden crate, fully insured. Lean it against a wall or hang it proud — either way, it brings a quiet, flowering elegance that the right mirror always does.

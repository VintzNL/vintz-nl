The Lady armchair is where modern Italian furniture begins. When Marco Zanuso presented it at the Milan Triennale in 1951, it won the gold medal — and it changed the way the world thought about seating. Revolutionary for its use of polyurethane foam and a new elastic band spring system, it proved that industrial materials could deliver comfort, beauty, and innovation simultaneously.

This pair is the 2015 Cassina re-edition, which honours Zanuso's original design while incorporating contemporary construction refinements. The steel frame supports poplar plywood armrests, while the seat combines polyurethane foam with polyester filling for the same generous comfort the original delivered. The wool upholstery adds warmth and texture, softening the sculptural form into something you genuinely want to spend time in.

Both chairs are in good original condition. The 2015 upholstery shows minimal wear, and the steel frames are pristine. As Cassina re-editions, they carry the manufacturer's authentication and the quality control that comes with one of Italy's most respected furniture houses.

Zanuso's career spanned architecture, industrial design, and furniture, and his collaborations with Cassina produced some of the most important Italian designs of the twentieth century. The Lady sits alongside his work for Arflex and Brionvega as a cornerstone of post-war Italian modernism — the moment when Italian design stepped onto the world stage and never left.

As a pair, these chairs create a conversation area, a reading corner, or a symmetrical anchor for a living room. The generous proportions invite lingering, while the elegant lines ensure they remain visually compelling from every angle.

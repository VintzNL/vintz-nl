The F773 is a contradiction made physical. Flowing, organic upholstery meets an industrial metal base, and from that tension emerges one of Artifort's most striking designs. Peter van der Ham created a chair that is equal parts sculpture and seating, a monumental form that commands attention in any interior.

The wave-shaped seat cradles the body in two distinct modes: an upright position for alert engagement and a reclined posture for contemplative lounging. This dual functionality is built into the organic curves themselves, not achieved through mechanical adjustment. The body simply finds its position within the generous contours. The Ploeg Monza Melange wool upholstery adds a sophisticated texture, its melange weave providing visual depth that complements the sculptural form.

The metal base acts as a deliberate counterpoint to the soft, flowing lines above. Like a decorative column or heavy pedestal, it grounds the organic shape with industrial precision. The contrast between sleek metal and enveloping upholstery creates a dynamic visual tension that keeps the eye moving.

Van der Ham designed the F773 in 1985, a period when Artifort was expanding its design vocabulary beyond the mid-century forms that had made its reputation. The Wave represented a new direction, one that embraced postmodern sculptural ambition while maintaining the comfort standards that had always defined the Maastricht manufacturer.

In a living room, a loft, or a design-conscious office, the F773 functions as much more than a chair. It is a centrepiece, a conversation starter, and a daily reminder that furniture, at its best, is art you can inhabit.

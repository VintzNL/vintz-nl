This Murano sommerso glass lamp base is a genuine piece of Italian glass artistry. The body is a heavy, hand-blown teardrop of crystal-clear glass, and suspended deep inside it — never touching the surface — floats a sculptural comma of cobalt blue edged in bright turquoise. That 'submerged' layering is the famous sommerso technique, and the effect is mesmerising: the colour seems to hang in mid-air, shifting and glowing as you move around it.

The form belongs to the great Murano tradition led by Flavio Poli, the artistic director who made sommerso world-famous at Seguso Vetri d'Arte in the 1950s and 60s. This piece is offered in that manner — unsigned, as many fine Murano pieces are, but with all the weight, clarity and confident asymmetry that define the best work of the period. It is mounted on a polished chrome oval base with a nickel fitting, ready to wire and use.

It is substantial in the hand and on a surface — 50 cm to the top of the fitting — and in very good condition, the glass bright and free of chips, a real sculptural object whether lit or not.

Please note: this is offered as the sculptural glass lamp base. The cream shade shown in some of the photographs is styling only, included to suggest scale and proportion, and is not part of the sale — we are happy to advise on shade options so it arrives exactly to your taste. It sits beautifully among the rest of our vintage design lighting, and beside other Murano craft such as our Carlo Moretti Murano vases.

Like every piece at Vintz, it was hand-picked for character and condition rather than restored, then shipped worldwide in a custom wooden crate, fully insured. Few materials hold the light like real Murano sommerso glass — switch it on and you will see exactly why collectors prize it.

There is something irresistible about the way 1930s French glassmakers captured light. This Art Deco pendant lamp, crafted in France around 1930, is made from moulded glass in a soft, translucent pink that transforms electric light into something warm, ambient and genuinely romantic.

The glass shade has been shaped into a floral form — petals and organic curves inspired by the natural motifs that defined Art Deco decorative arts. When illuminated, the pink glass produces a gentle, diffused glow that softens every surface it touches. Brass fittings connect the shade to its hanging chain, adding a warm metallic accent that complements the rosy glass. The overall drop of 65 centimetres and width of 35 centimetres make it suitable for a dining table, a bedroom, or an entrance hall.

This pendant lamp is in good condition and fully functional. The glass shade is intact without chips or cracks, the brass fittings are secure, and the lamp is wired for standard use. Minor wear to the brass is consistent with nearly a century of life and adds patina rather than diminishing the piece.

The 1930s represented a golden age of French decorative lighting. The widespread availability of electricity from the 1920s onward had spurred enormous innovation in glass design, and French craftsmen led the way — moulding delicate glass into floral, geometric and figurative forms that turned functional light fixtures into objects of genuine beauty. This pendant belongs to that tradition, reflecting the Art Deco movement's characteristic interest in nature-inspired ornament rendered through modern materials and techniques.

Hung above a table or in a quiet corner, this lamp does what the best vintage lighting always does — it changes the character of a room. The soft pink glow creates atmosphere that no contemporary fixture can quite match, bringing a piece of 1930s France into your daily life.

This is an original Andy Warhol exhibition poster, printed for his 1975 show at the Galleria Civica d'Arte Moderna in the Palazzo dei Diamanti, Ferrara — 'comune di ferrara · palazzo dei diamanti · 26 ottobre – 8 dicembre 1975', exactly as it reads across the top. It is a genuine period piece of Pop Art history, not a modern reprint, and it still carries the presence of the moment it was made for.

The image is one of Warhol's most striking: a portrait from his Ladies and Gentlemen series, the bold 1975 cycle in which he turned New York drag performers into glamorous, monumental icons. Hand-screened colour does what only Warhol could — slabs of crimson and gold cut across the face, the gaze half-turned, beautiful and confrontational at once, with his name set large in red beneath.

It is ready to hang, the paper showing the honest character you want from a fifty-year-old poster: a little rippling, a few light surface marks, nothing that distracts from the image. We describe it candidly as in good condition for its age — a poster that was clearly displayed and loved, not stored flat in a drawer.

Original exhibition posters like this sit right at the meeting point of fine art and graphic design, and they bring instant edge to a wall — a hallway, a studio, a gallery-style interior. It pairs naturally with bold Italian design pieces such as our Carlo Moretti Murano vases, or the rest of our curated vintage objects.

Like every piece at Vintz, it was hand-picked for character and authenticity rather than restored, then shipped worldwide in a custom wooden crate, fully insured. Hang it where it can hold a wall on its own — because a real 1975 Warhol poster always does.

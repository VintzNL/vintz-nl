West German ceramics from the 1970s have become some of the most collectible pottery of the twentieth century, and this bold red vase shows exactly why. Standing 44 centimetres tall, it has the scale and presence to anchor an entire room.

The glaze is the first thing you notice. A deep, saturated red that catches light with an almost liquid quality, applied with the kind of confidence that only handwork produces. The surface texture varies subtly across the body of the vase, evidence of the maker's hand and the unpredictable alchemy of the kiln. No two firings are ever identical, and this piece wears its individuality proudly.

The form is generous and sculptural, with proportions that balance weight and elegance. At 26 centimetres in diameter, it has real visual gravity. The condition is clean, with no chips or cracks, the ceramic body intact throughout.

The West German pottery movement of the 1960s and 1970s produced an extraordinary range of forms and glazes. Factories like Scheurich, Bay Keramik, and dozens of smaller workshops created pieces that ranged from the restrained to the wildly experimental. This vase sits firmly in the bold camp, its saturated colour and substantial form making it a statement piece rather than a quiet accent.

Whether displayed alone on a sideboard or grouped with other ceramics, this vase brings warmth, colour, and a tangible connection to one of the most vibrant periods in European decorative arts.
